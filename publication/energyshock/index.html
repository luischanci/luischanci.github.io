Energy Shocks, Firms' Productivity and Markups

Luis Chancí
February 2019

Abstract

In this paper, I investigate how firms respond to electricity price shocks. Using a natural experiment - the 2004 Argentine energy crisis,- variations in energy prices interacted with the shares of fuels used at industry-level (shift-share instruments), and a structural estimation approach for the production function, the paper studies how electricity prices affect firms’ markups and productivity. Preliminary results show that a 10% increase in electricity prices leads to a 16% decrease in markups, and about 0.05% decrease in productivity.
